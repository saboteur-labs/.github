# Saboteur Labs

_Engineering systems and experiences with intent._

---

Saboteur Labs is the open-source and developer infrastructure arm of [Saboteur](https://github.com/saboteur-works) — a creator-first software company building tools and platforms for independent artists and authors.

The tools here exist because we needed them. Each one started as a real problem encountered while building real products, and ended up useful enough to stand on its own.

## What we build

### Tools

We build small, focused, deterministic tools. The philosophy is consistent across everything here:

- Do one thing and do it well
- Fail loudly and predictably rather than silently and mysteriously
- Produce output that automation can trust
- Leave the system exactly as you found it

If a tool modifies something it should not, produces inconsistent output, or obscures what went wrong, it does not belong here.

### Knowledge

Useful knowledge does not become less useful by being shared. We document what we learn in public — the same standard we hold our tools to.

This means what we publish here is not polished curriculum or thought leadership. It is working knowledge: patterns we have tested, decisions we have made, tradeoffs we have encountered. If it helps us build better software, it belongs here. If it might help you, it is yours.

We also hold ourselves to a specific kind of honesty about what we do not know:

- What is true today may not be true tomorrow. We try to note when something is uncertain, provisional, or likely to change.
- Some of what we share will be wrong. We would rather publish imperfect knowledge openly than withhold it until it is perfect.
- We draw from many sources — documentation, experience, experimentation, and AI-assisted research. We try to say which is which.
- Constructive corrections are welcome. Our goal is not to be the best authority. It is to share the best knowledge available to us and improve it when we are shown a better way.

_This standard applies to all knowledge repositories under saboteur-labs, present and future._

## Projects

### Open-Source Tools

Varsentry
Deterministic CLI for validating .env files, runtime versions, and configuration integrity before your application runs. Built for local development, CI pipelines, and production preflight checks. MIT-licensed.

### Open Knowledge

ai-workflows
Documented patterns for working with LLMs in development workflows. A living reference, not a framework.

Omoikane (specification stage)
A repository-backed, AI-augmented research and learning tool. Named after the Shinto deity of wisdom and deliberation. The central commitment: deliberation over speed. It is better to surface a well-described gap than to fill it with a plausible-sounding answer.

## Open source philosophy

The core of everything here is and will remain free and open. Where commercial extensions exist, they add capability — they do not restrict what was already available.

We do not build complexity as lock-in.
